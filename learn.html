Types of Homes

Single-Family Home: A standalone dwelling designed for one family. It typically has a front and backyard, offering more privacy and space compared to multi-family homes.

Apartment: A unit within a multi-story building. It's often part of a larger complex with shared amenities. Apartments are ideal for individuals, couples, or small families.

Condominium (Condo): Similar to an apartment but owned rather than rented. Owners share common areas and pay fees for maintenance. Condos provide a balance of ownership and communal living.

Townhouse: Attached homes in a row, sharing walls with neighbors. Often multi-story with a small yard or patio. Combines aspects of single-family and condo living.

Duplex: A building divided into two separate units. Can be owner-occupied or used for rental income.

Mobile Home/Manufactured Home: A prefabricated home that can be transported to a specific location. Typically located in mobile home parks or on private land.

Cooperative (Co-op): Residents own shares in the cooperative corporation rather than owning individual units. Co-op boards often have a say in who can become a resident.

Bungalow: A single-story house with a sloping roof and front porch. Often chosen for its accessibility and cozy design.

Cottage: A small, cozy house, usually located in a rural or scenic location. Perfect for those seeking a retreat from city life.

Mansion: A large, luxurious home often associated with wealth. Features extensive amenities and space.

Ranch-style Home: A single-story home with a long, low-profile design. Typically features an open floor plan.

Colonial Home: A classic American home with symmetrical design. Often has two or more stories with a centered front door.

Victorian Home: Known for intricate, ornate designs and multiple stories. Often features decorative details and asymmetrical architecture.

Cabin: A small, rustic home, usually located in a natural setting.

Earthship: An eco-friendly, self-sustaining home built using natural and recycled materials. Designed to minimize environmental impact.
